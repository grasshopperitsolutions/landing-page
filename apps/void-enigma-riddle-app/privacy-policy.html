2. Data Collection and Anonymity

The App is designed for anonymity and **does not** collect, store, or transmit any Personally Identifiable Information (PII) about you.

Specifically, the App:

  • Does **not** require user accounts, registration, names, email addresses, or phone numbers.
  • Does **not** collect precise location data, contacts, photos, or messages from your device.
  • Does **not** use third-party analytics or advertising tracking SDKs.

3. Use of the Gemini API

The core function of the App is to generate riddles using the Google Gemini API. This interaction involves transmitting data outside the App to Google's servers.

  • Data Transmitted: When you request a new riddle, the App sends a brief, non-personal text prompt to the Gemini API (e.g., "Generate a fun, short riddle"). This request **does not** include any information that identifies you or your device.
  • API Key Security: The developer API key used to communicate with Gemini is secured within the application's code and is never exposed to the end-user.
  • Data Handling by Google: Your interaction with the Gemini API is subject to Google's privacy policies. We encourage you to review the Google AI Privacy Hub for details on their data practices.

4. Data Storage and Retention

All data storage related to your activity is minimal and temporary:

  • In-App Memory: The App temporarily stores the current riddle and answer in your device's memory for display purposes. It also maintains an in-memory history of recently seen riddles to minimize repetition within a single session.
  • Data Deletion: This temporary session data is immediately cleared when you fully close the App or explicitly reset the history. No long-term persistent storage (like cookies, local storage, or databases) is used to track session history or store riddles between launches.

5. Children’s Privacy

The App is a riddle game intended for a general audience and is designed to generate content suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect any personal information from children under the age of 13. If a parent or guardian becomes aware that their child has provided us with personal information, they should contact us immediately at the email provided in Section 1.
